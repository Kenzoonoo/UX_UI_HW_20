D.O.G.S.

Dogs Of A Good Society, or D.O.G.S., is a 501-c non-profit, based in
Minnesota. Their mission is to provide financial resources to animal rescues
and shelters, specifically for emergency medical issues and spaying and
neutering. The organization hosts fundraisers and does grant writing but
also relies on monthly donations to fund their work.



USER RESEARCH

Stakeholder Interviews

We started by setting up and interviewing two of the board members for Dogs Of A Good Society and organized the notes into an affinity diagram. This helped us understand what their main priorities and pain points were. Some of their pain points included users not knowing that they help more than just dogs and that their assistance form is an external link to google and they would prefer it to be in-house. From there, we created a proto persona to help us get an understanding of what our assumptions may be in regards to the users.

Proto Persona

Our proto persona’s name is Braylee, a 36 year old accountant who volunteers for her local rescue. Her main priority is to find financial support to cover the vet care of a dog that came into their rescue with an emergency medical issue.

User interviews

We then created a user interview plan based on some of the pain points discovered during the stakeholder interviews. Based on the empathy mapping session, we did some feature prioritization work to determine which areas we wanted to tackle based on feasibility, the stakeholder input, and what we learned from the user interviews.

PROBLEM STATMENT


D.O.G.S was designed to achieve the goal of prodicing financial support to animal rescues in order to save animals in need. We have observed that our website isn't meeting the users need to donate or find assistance, which is causing a high bounce rate to our buisness.

Testing and Iteration

Site Mapping

We did some very basic card sorting to organize the three sections that didn’t have any links in version 1 (as can be seen) and placed them into related sections as can be seen in version 2.

Mid-fi User Testing

These are affinity diagrams for the mid-fidelity testing. We have the desktop notes on the left and the mobile notes on the right. One of the takeaways we got from that was to ensure the banner had a clear Call to Action, which we did incorporate into the high fidelity.

High-fi A/B Testing

We created two high fidelity versions for the AB testing. The main difference between them is how we labeled the assistance form and the image we used, with all of the users preferring the B version, which had the assistance application labeled and an image of a veterinarian. We also put in a carousel image on the hero banner with images of the president’s dogs, but also put in images of cats, rabbits, and guinea pigs, to indicate it wasn’t just an organization that helped dogs, despite the name of the org.

Final Prototype


D.O.G.S. is a wonderful non-profit with an admiral goal. Due to the existing website and lack of structure, stakeholders noted a high bounce rate. Through user interviews and testing we were able to highlight several user pain points that would go on to be implimented into our final responsive prototype.
